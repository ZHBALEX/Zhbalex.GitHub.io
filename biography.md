## Biography

I am a master's student in Mechanical Engineering at Johns Hopkins University. My work combines computational fluid dynamics, biofluid mechanics, and scientific computing in the Flow Physics and Computational Lab, where I am advised by [Dr. Rajat Mittal](https://me.jhu.edu/faculty/rajat-mittal/) and [Dr. Jung-Hee Seo](https://engineering.jhu.edu/fsag/people/faculty/jung_hee_seo/).

My current research focuses on pancreatic duct flow and the development of integrated computational tools to help identify chronic pancreatitis associated with pancreatic ductal hypertension. The work connects MRI-based reconstruction, geometric analysis, CFD simulation, pressure prediction, and physics-informed learning.

Before Johns Hopkins, I completed my undergraduate studies at [Southern Illinois University](https://siu.edu) and [Shenyang Aerospace University](https://en.sau.edu.cn), specializing in Aircraft Powerplant Engineering. My training covered fluid dynamics, energy systems, mechanical design, and hands-on propulsion engine assembly and maintenance.
